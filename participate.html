Participate

Participate in research at the FLAME Lab!

Are you or your child curious about how the mind works? We invite children, teens, and adults to take part in research on how we remember, imagine, and think about the world!

By joining a study, you'll help us better understand how the brain and thinking change as people grow and contribute to discoveries that could support learning and well-being in childhood and beyond.

Want to get involved or learn more? Click here to share your info so we can reach out about future study opportunities - no commitment required!

What do our studies involve?

We study how people of different ages remember the past and imagine the future. Participants might complete interactive tasks like computer games, storytelling, puzzles, or optional brain imaging (MRI). Our studies are designed to be fun and age-appropriate and your time is always appreciated and compensated with cash and sometimes bonus prizes. See the FAQ section below for more information.

Interested in participating?

We're always looking for families and individuals to join our studies! By signing up, you'll be added to our contact list and notified about research opportunities that may be a good fit for you or your child. There's no commitment—just a chance to learn more and take part if you're interested. Click here to sign up, and if you ever change your mind, you can opt out at any time.

Frequently Asked Questions Who Can Participate?

We welcome participants of all ages! We're especially excited to hear from families with children, as many of our studies focus on how thinking develops over time.

What will we do during the visit?

You or your child will complete fun, research-based activities like memory games, puzzles, or short interviews. Some studies may include a visit to a mock MRI scanner (a kid-friendly pretend version), or an optional real MRI session.

How long does a study visit take?

Most visits take 1 to 2 hours, depending on the study. We'll always let you know what to expect ahead of time—and we do our best to keep things fun and flexible.

Will we be paid?

Yes! Participants usually earn $10-$25 per hour, and younger kids may also receive stickers, small prizes, or certificates of participation.

Can I stay with my child?

Absolutely! We ask that you remain nearby, whether the study is in person or virtual. For in-person visits, parents, siblings, or another close support person are welcome to stay in our family-friendly waiting room just next to the lab. We want everyone to feel comfortable and supported throughout the visit.

Is participation voluntary?

Yes. Participation is always voluntary, and you or your child can stop at any time. We're happy to answer questions before, during, or after your visit.

Where is the lab? What's the space like?

The FLAME Lab is located in the Behavioral Sciences Building at the University of Illinois Chicago (UIC). We're easy to get to - just off the main road, near public transit (including a Blue Line stop), and close to nearby parking.

Our address is: B152, 1077 W Harrison St, Chicago, IL 60607 and we offer:

  • A family-friendly waiting room
  • Guest Wi-Fi
  • Parking vouchers for the Harrison Street Parking Structure located at 1100 W. Harrison Street.

If your visit includes an MRI, that portion takes place just down the street at UIC's Center for Magnetic Resonance Research (CMRR). The CMRR is located at 2242 W. Harrison Street, Suite 103, Chicago, IL 60612, and it offers:

  • A comfortable, family-friendly space
  • Guest Wi-Fi
  • Free, reserved parking for participants
